Definitions and Data Sources

The Global Fund (GF) provides 56% of all international financing for malaria programs (39% of total available resources) and has invested more than 14.7 billion USD in malaria control programs. Since 2020, the Global Fund has provided additional funding through the COVID-19 Response Mechanism to countries to mitigate the impact of COVID-19 on the malaria response. GF contribution is particularly important for Africa, a continent which accounts for over 90% of the global malaria burden.

The formula for allocating funds to country programs is based on each country’s disease burden (as recommended by technical partners) and economic capacity (measured by GNI per capita). It also takes into account maximum and minimum shares, adjustments for other external financing, and needs for scale up and paced reductions. Importantly, unused funds remaining at the original grant end date will be returned to the Global Fund and used for portfolio optimization investments through the allocation period. It is therefore important to keep regular track of the proportion of unused funds for each grant. The scorecard at the end of this dashboard provides a proxy to monitor grant absorbance. It is defined as the ratio between cumulated disbursements over last two quarters and cumated budgets planned for the same period. To better analyze this proxy we need to take into account the date of the end of the grant.

This Dashboard summarize investments in Malaria Programs by the GF, using publicly available GF Data accessed trough the Global Fund Data API.
Definitions and Data sources

In this Dashboard we provide information on GF grants to Malaria at the country level. The filter box on the top allows selecting one or multiple countries for analysis. 

The Cumulative Budgets vs. Cumulative Disbursements for Active Grants Plot, is aimed to track potential Gaps between quarterly budgets and disboursements. It can be seen as a dynamic proxy for Grant absorbance (formally defined in terms of the expenditure) or to monitor unused funds per Grant. Note that unused funds remaining at the original grant end date will be returned to the Global Fund and used for portfolio optimization investments through the allocation period. Therefore, the ratio disboursements to budgets should be as close as possible to one particularly near the grant end date. Quarterly ratios are shown on the bar plot entitled "Cumulative Budgets vs. Cumulative Disbursements for Active Grants". Note that this ratio can be very low if the grant budgeted amount is much larger than the signed amount. Therefore, the table at the end of the Dashboard also includes the disbursements to committments ratio that can be used as an alternative but less dynamic proxy.
